Overview

The Walmart Sales Analysis project focused on performing business-driven analysis on Walmartâ€™s sales data to uncover trends, customer purchasing behaviors, and performance insights across different product categories. The goal was to provide actionable insights that could drive retail strategy and improve decision-making at the organizational level.

Problem Statement

Retail businesses like Walmart rely on data to assess product performance, optimize inventory management, and understand customer purchasing trends. The aim was to clean, transform, and analyze large datasets to extract business intelligence that can guide operational decisions.

Approach & Methodology

  1. Data Cleaning & Transformation:
    • Used SQL for initial data extraction and transformation, cleaning the dataset by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and standardizing columns.
    • Leveraged Python (Pandas) for further data manipulation, including aggregating sales data by region, product category, and payment type.
  2. SQL Analysis & Custom Metrics:
    • Built complex SQL queries to extract actionable insights like:
      • Total sales per region
      • Product category performance comparison
      • Payment method usage by customer demographics
      • Designed custom metrics like sales per square foot and average transaction value to evaluate store performance.
  3. KPI Generation & Visualization:
    • Used SQLAlchemy to connect MySQL databases with Python for seamless data import and transformation.
    • Developed clear, actionable KPIs such as:
      • Year-over-year growth in sales
      • Top-performing product categories
      • Cross-region sales trends to guide inventory distribution.

Key Outcomes & Insights

  • Uncovered a significant trend in regional sales performance, with certain areas consistently outperforming others.
  • Identified underperforming product categories and recommended actions for targeted marketing and inventory adjustments.
  • Provided a deeper understanding of customer payment preferences, suggesting potential optimizations for payment systems in-store and online.

Key Learnings & Highlights

  • Enhanced understanding of SQL and data modeling for large-scale retail datasets.
  • Developed strong business acumen for extracting insights from data that have direct commercial value.
  • Practiced using Python and SQL in tandem to streamline workflows for complex data analysis tasks.
