Overview

The Mental Well-Being Analysis project focused on examining behavioral and lifestyle data to understand key factors influencing mental health and overall well-being. The objective was to identify meaningful patterns linking daily habits, stress indicators, and emotional states to mental well-being outcomes. The analysis emphasized structured exploration and contextual interpretation to ensure insights were actionable and grounded in real-world behavior.

Problem Statement

Mental well-being is shaped by a combination of lifestyle choices, behavioral patterns, and stress-related factors, yet these relationships are often complex and interdependent. The challenge was to analyze multi-dimensional data to uncover patterns that explain variations in mental well-being levels and identify potential drivers of stress and balance.

Approach & Methodology

  1. Data Cleaning & Preparation:
    • Cleaned and structured raw datasets by handling missing values, standardizing features, and validating data consistency.
    • Prepared behavioral and lifestyle variables for exploratory analysis.
  2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):
    • Analyzed distributions and relationships across mental well-being indicators.
    • Explored correlations between sleep patterns, workload, stress levels, and emotional health.
  3. Insight Generation & Interpretation:
    • Identified behavioral patterns associated with higher and lower well-being scores.
    • Focused on contextual interpretation to ensure findings aligned with realistic behavioral logic.
  4. Visualization & Communication:
    • Used visual analytics to communicate trends and comparisons clearly.
    • Framed insights to support awareness and data-informed decision-making.

Key Outcomes & Insights

  • Observed strong associations between consistent routines and higher mental well-being indicators.
  • Identified stress-related behavioral patterns linked to lower well-being scores.
  • Highlighted segments that could benefit from targeted lifestyle or behavioral interventions.

Key Learnings & Highlights

  • Strengthened ability to analyze sensitive, human-centric datasets responsibly.
  • Improved proficiency in exploratory analysis for multi-factor behavioral data.
  • Reinforced the importance of combining data insights with judgment and context.
