Overview

This project focused on designing a hybrid risk assessment framework to help stakeholders in construction projects evaluate, prioritize, and mitigate potential risks. The goal was to deliver a data-driven and probabilistic solution for quantifying the impact and likelihood of various risk factors.

Problem Statement

Construction projects often face significant uncertainty due to fluctuating costs, delays, environmental factors, and workforce management issues. A structured framework was needed to assess risks more objectively and support better decision-making.

Approach & Methodology

  1. Risk Identification & Structuring:
    • Conducted literature research and stakeholder interviews to identify key risk parameters (e.g., material delays, budget overrun, labor shortages).
    • Structured risks into quantifiable variables and defined them using both qualitative and quantitative metrics.
  2. Decision Tree Regression (ML Component):
    • Used historical data to train a Decision Tree Regression model that predicted potential cost/time overruns based on early-stage project inputs.
    • Enabled scenario-based forecasting by simulating changes in inputs and observing the projected risk outcome.
  3. Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP):
    • Applied AHP to assign weights to different risk factors through pairwise comparisons.
    • Allowed domain experts to bring subjectivity into the model in a mathematically consistent way.
  4. Monte Carlo Simulation:
    • Ran thousands of simulation trials to model the probabilistic behavior of the overall risk.
    • Provided output in terms of probability distributions, helping users understand best-case, worst-case, and most likely risk scenarios.

Key Outcomes & Insights

  • Created a structured framework that blends machine learning, expert judgment, and simulation for holistic risk assessment.
  • Visualized risk probability curves and risk impact matrices using Matplotlib for decision support.
  • Enabled construction managers to better anticipate risk hotspots and plan contingencies accordingly.

Key Learnings & Highlights

  • Gained hands-on experience combining predictive modeling and multi-criteria decision-making techniques.
  • Enhanced understanding of uncertainty modeling and its real-world application in project management.
  • Demonstrated the value of hybrid approaches in solving complex business problems.
