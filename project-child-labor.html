Overview

This initiative examined child labor not as an isolated social issue, but as a complex systems challenge shaped by economic pressure, education gaps, policy limitations, and community dynamics. Leading a six-member interdisciplinary team, the project applied the full Design Thinking framework to uncover root causes, align stakeholders, and design structured intervention pathways. The objective was to create a scalable, community-supported Child Safety & Education Continuity model that balances analytical rigor with human-centered insight.

Problem Framing

Traditional approaches to child labor often focus on enforcement or short-term rescue interventions, without addressing systemic drivers such as household income dependency, school accessibility, or community-level support structures. The challenge was to redefine the problem in a way that integrates social realities, policy constraints, and practical implementation considerations into a cohesive strategy.

Approach & Methodology

  1. Empathize & Context Mapping:
    • Conducted stakeholder mapping to identify affected groups including children, families, local educators, and policymakers.
    • Developed detailed personas to capture lived experiences, behavioral motivations, and structural constraints.
  2. Define & Problem Structuring:
    • Created affinity maps to synthesize qualitative insights into recurring patterns and systemic gaps.
    • Formulated policy-oriented problem statements that balanced humanitarian priorities with operational feasibility.
  3. Ideate & Systems Design:
    • Designed a Child Safety & Education Continuity System integrating community oversight, education retention mechanisms, and structured monitoring processes.
    • Evaluated solution feasibility across scalability, stakeholder alignment, and long-term sustainability dimensions.
  4. Prototype & Validation:
    • Developed conceptual frameworks and process flows illustrating implementation pathways.
    • Tested assumptions through feedback loops and iterative refinement discussions within the team.

Key Outcomes & Strategic Insights

  • Reframed child labor as a systems-level issue requiring coordinated intervention rather than isolated enforcement.
  • Demonstrated how structured analytical reasoning enhances human-centered solution design.
  • Proposed a scalable intervention framework balancing empathy, policy alignment, and operational practicality.

Leadership & Professional Impact

  • Led interdisciplinary collaboration across six members with diverse perspectives and working styles.
  • Translated complex qualitative insights into structured, decision-oriented frameworks.
  • Strengthened capabilities in stakeholder alignment, systems thinking, and strategic problem-solving.
